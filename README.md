#### [Top CTF platforms](https://snyk.io/articles/ctf/platforms-practice/#top)

* [CTFtime](https://ctftime.org/): Not a platform in itself, but a great resource to keep track of upcoming CTFs and see team rankings.
* [Hack The Box (HTB)](https://www.hackthebox.eu/): A platform that offers various penetration testing labs and challenges ranging from beginner to advanced. Users have to 'hack' their way in just to get an account!
* [TryHackMe](https://tryhackme.com/): Offers learning paths and challenges across a range of cybersecurity topics and difficulty levels. It's user-friendly for beginners too.
* [Root Me](https://www.root-me.org/): A platform that offers various hacking challenges and virtual environments to practice various penetration testing skills.
* [PicoCTF](https://picoctf.org/): Hosted by Carnegie Mellon University, it's especially beginner-friendly with a storyline and progressively harder challenges.
* [OverTheWire](https://overthewire.org/wargames/): It’s best known for its beginner-friendly 'Bandit' series, but also offers more advanced war games.
* [Hack This Site](https://www.hackthissite.org/): An older platform but still has a variety of challenges, ranging from basic to realistic missions.
* [VulnHub](https://www.vulnhub.com/): Offers VMs (Virtual Machines) to download and hack at your own pace. It's great for practicing offline and setting up your own lab.
* [RingZer0 Team Online CTF](https://ringzer0ctf.com/): Features a variety of challenges, from codebreaking to shellcoding.
* **CyberSecLabs** : A platform for beginners to intermediate users to practice their penetration testing skills by providing a variety of labs.
* [PentesterLab](https://www.pentesterlab.com/): Offers hands-on labs and exercises to learn web hacking, covering various vulnerabilities.
* [CTFLearn](https://ctflearn.com/): Another beginner-friendly platform with a range of binary, web, and crypto challenges.
* **365 Days of CTF** : A platform offering a daily CTF challenge.
